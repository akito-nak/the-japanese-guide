# Internet & Slang — wwww and Other Mysteries

> *"Japanese internet culture has evolved its own language. Understanding it unlocks a massive slice of modern Japan — and honestly, it's hilarious."*

Japanese internet slang is rich, weird, creative, and changes fast. A lot of it originated on 2channel (now 5channel), Japan's legendary anonymous bulletin board, spread through Twitter/X and LINE, and eventually became mainstream. Understanding it helps with anime, manga, social media, and just... being on the internet in Japanese.

---

## The Basics: wwww

Let's start with the most iconic.

**w** = laughing (like LOL)

The origin: **笑い** (warai) = laughter. The kanji 笑 (wara) → romanized as "w" → used as shorthand. Stack them for intensity:

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| w | lol |
| ww | haha |
| www | lmao |
| wwww | I am losing it |
| wwwwwwwww | I am dying |

Sometimes written as **草** (kusa = grass) — because a field of w's looks like grass. "草生えた" (kusa haeta = grass grew) = something is really funny.

## LINE Sticker Culture

LINE is the dominant messaging app in Japan (like WhatsApp in the US). Its sticker system has heavily influenced how Japanese people communicate online — and created its own vocabulary:

- **既読スルー (kidoku suru)** — "Read-ignore" — seeing a message and deliberately not responding. One of the most discussed concepts in modern Japanese relationships.
- **ライン交換 (LINE kōkan)** — Exchanging LINE IDs — the Japanese equivalent of "can I get your number?"
- **ブロック (burokku)** — Being blocked on LINE — quite serious socially

## Numbers as Words (Goroawase — 語呂合わせ)

Japanese wordplay using numbers is popular because each digit can be read multiple ways:

| Number | Possible readings |
|---|---|
| 1 | ichi, i, hi |
| 2 | ni, fu, tsu |
| 3 | san, mi, sa |
| 4 | shi, yo, yon |
| 5 | go, ko, o |
| 6 | roku, ro, mu |
| 7 | shichi, nana, na |
| 8 | hachi, ha, ya, pa |
| 9 | ku, kyu, ko |
| 0 | zero, re, o, wa |

Famous examples:
- **110** (hyaku-jū) = police emergency number but also read as one-one-zero
- **4649** = よろしく (yoroshiku) — read as yo-ro-shi-ku
- **0840** = おはよう (ohayō) — read as o-ha-yo-u
- **724106** = 何もしない (nanimo shinai) = doing nothing

These show up in phone numbers, product names, and internet jokes constantly.

## The Internet Gave Japanese New Kanji Uses

Creative kanji repurposing happens constantly online:

| Usage | Traditional meaning | Internet meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 草 (kusa) | grass | lol (see wwww section) |
| 神 (kami) | god | god-tier, incredible |
| 闇 (yami) | darkness | dark/disturbing depth |
| 鬼 (oni) | demon/ogre | "super~" (鬼速い = insanely fast) |
| 爆 (baku) | explosion | "explosively ~" (爆笑 = burst out laughing) |
| 秒 (byō) | second | "instantly" / "already done" |
| 廃人 (haijin) | ruined person | someone who plays a game unhealthily much |
