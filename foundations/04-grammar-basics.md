# Grammar Basics — How Japanese Sentences Actually Work

> *"The first time you realize Japanese puts the verb at the end, your brain reboots. The second time, it feels completely logical."*

Japanese grammar is simultaneously simpler and more different than English grammar. No gender. No plural forms for most nouns. No articles (no "a," "an," or "the"). But: verbs go at the end, particles mark every noun's role, and politeness is baked into the verb itself.

Let's break it down.

---

## The Core Structure: Subject-Object-Verb (SOV)

English: **Subject + Verb + Object**
> I eat sushi.

Japanese: **Subject + Object + Verb**
> 私は寿司を食べます。
> (Watashi wa sushi wo tabemasu.)
> I [topic] sushi [object] eat.

The verb **always goes at the end** of the sentence (in standard Japanese). Adjectives, time expressions, location phrases — they all come before the verb. Get used to this, and everything else will fall into place.

---

## Particles — The Traffic Signs of Japanese

Particles are small hiragana attached to nouns that tell you *what role* that noun plays in the sentence. They're the most important grammatical concept in Japanese.

### The Essential Particles

| Particle | Role | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **は** (wa) | Topic marker — "as for X" | 私**は**学生です。I am a student. |
| **が** (ga) | Subject marker — emphasis, new info | 猫**が**好きです。I like cats (specifically). |
| **を** (wo/o) | Object marker — what the verb acts on | 水**を**飲む。Drink water. |
| **に** (ni) | Direction, location (existence), time | 東京**に**行く。Go to Tokyo. |
| **で** (de) | Location (action), means/method | 電車**で**来た。Came by train. |
| **と** (to) | And (nouns), together with | 友達**と**行く。Go with a friend. |
| **の** (no) | Possessive, noun modifier | 私**の**本。My book. |
| **も** (mo) | Also, too | 私**も**行く。I'm going too. |
| **か** (ka) | Question marker | これは何**か**？What is this? |
| **ね** (ne) | Seeking agreement — "right?" | 寒いです**ね**。It's cold, isn't it. |
| **よ** (yo) | Assertive — "I'm telling you" | 知ってます**よ**。I know, you know. |
| **から** (kara) | From, because | 東京**から**来た。Came from Tokyo. |
| **まで** (made) | Until, up to | 5時**まで**働く。Work until 5. |
| **より** (yori) | Than (comparison) | 猫**より**犬が好き。I like dogs more than cats. |

### は (wa) vs. が (ga) — The Famous Nightmare

This is the most debated grammar point in Japanese. Here's the practical version:

- **は** introduces a topic you're talking *about* — general, already established context
- **が** marks the specific subject in a clause — new information, contrast, emphasis

> 象は鼻が長い。
> "As for elephants, (their) noses are long."
> (は marks "elephants" as the topic; が marks "noses" as the subject of 長い)

> 誰が来た？田中さんが来た。
> "Who came? Tanaka-san came."
> (が is used for new information answering a specific question)

Don't over-stress this distinction early on. Natives will understand you either way. Nuance comes with reading real Japanese.

## Verbs — Three Groups and a Lot of Conjugation

All Japanese verbs end in **う (u)** sounds in their dictionary form. They fall into three groups:

### Group 1: U-verbs (五段動詞 — Godan)
The stem changes depending on the form. The final syllable changes across the u-row:

| Dictionary | Meaning | Masu form | Nai form | Te form |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 書く (kaku) | write | 書きます | 書かない | 書いて |
| 話す (hanasu) | speak | 話します | 話さない | 話して |
| 飲む (nomu) | drink | 飲みます | 飲まない | 飲んで |
| 読む (yomu) | read | 読みます | 読まない | 読んで |
| 行く (iku) | go | 行きます | 行かない | 行って *(irregular te)* |
| 買う (kau) | buy | 買います | 買わない | 買って |
| 待つ (matsu) | wait | 待ちます | 待たない | 待って |

### Group 2: Ru-verbs (一段動詞 — Ichidan)
The stem doesn't change — just drop る and add endings:

| Dictionary | Meaning | Masu form | Nai form | Te form |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 食べる (taberu) | eat | 食べます | 食べない | 食べて |
| 見る (miru) | see | 見ます | 見ない | 見て |
| 起きる (okiru) | wake up | 起きます | 起きない | 起きて |
| 寝る (neru) | sleep | 寝ます | 寝ない | 寝て |
| 教える (oshieru) | teach | 教えます | 教えない | 教えて |

> **Warning:** Some verbs that *look* like ru-verbs are actually u-verbs. 帰る (kaeru = return home), 走る (hashiru = run), 知る (shiru = know). These fool everyone.

### Group 3: Irregular Verbs
Only two — but you'll use them constantly:

| Dictionary | Meaning | Masu form | Nai form | Te form |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| する (suru) | do | します | しない | して |
| くる (kuru) | come | きます | こない | きて |

## Verb Conjugation Chart

The four most essential forms to learn first:

| Form | Ending | Usage | Example (食べる) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dictionary** | -ru / -u | Casual present/future | 食べる (taberu) |
| **Masu** | -masu | Polite present/future | 食べます (tabemasu) |
| **Nai** | -nai | Negative | 食べない (tabenai) |
| **Ta** | -ta / -da | Past (casual) | 食べた (tabeta) |
| **Mashita** | -mashita | Past (polite) | 食べました (tabemashita) |
| **Te** | -te / -de | Connecting form | 食べて (tabete) |
| **Tara** | -tara | Conditional "if/when" | 食べたら (tabetara) |
| **Ba** | -eba / -reba | Conditional (hypothetical) | 食べれば (tabereba) |

## Adjectives — Two Flavors

### い-adjectives (i-adjectives)
End in い in their base form. Conjugate by changing the い:

| Form | Example (大きい = big) |
|---|---|
| Present positive | 大きい (ōkii) |
| Present negative | 大きくない (ōkiku nai) |
| Past positive | 大きかった (ōkikatta) |
| Past negative | 大きくなかった (ōkiku nakatta) |
| Before noun | 大きい犬 (big dog) |
| As predicate | 犬は大きい (The dog is big) |

Common い-adjectives: 大きい (big), 小さい (small), 高い (tall/expensive), 安い (cheap), 新しい (new), 古い (old), 難しい (difficult), 易しい (easy), 楽しい (fun), 悲しい (sad), 暑い (hot), 寒い (cold)

### な-adjectives (na-adjectives)
Essentially nouns that can modify other nouns. Add な before a noun, add です to make a predicate:

| Form | Example (きれい = pretty/clean) |
|---|---|
| Present positive | きれいです (kirei desu) |
| Present negative | きれいじゃない / きれいではない |
| Past positive | きれいでした |
| Past negative | きれいじゃなかった |
| Before noun | きれいな花 (pretty flower) |

Common な-adjectives: きれい (pretty/clean), 元気 (healthy/energetic), 好き (like), 嫌い (dislike), 有名 (famous), 便利 (convenient), 静か (quiet), 賑やか (lively), 丁寧 (polite), 親切 (kind)

> **Trap:** いい (good) is an い-adjective but conjugates irregularly: negative is よくない, past is よかった.

## The Copula: です and だ

The equivalent of "to be" in Japanese:

| Style | Present | Negative | Past | Past negative |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Polite | です | じゃないです / ではありません | でした | じゃなかったです |
| Casual | だ | じゃない | だった | じゃなかった |

> 学生です。(Gakusei desu.) = I am a student. (polite)
> 学生だ。(Gakusei da.) = I'm a student. (casual)
> 学生じゃない。(Gakusei ja nai.) = I'm not a student. (casual)

## Question Formation — Just Add か

Unlike English, you don't rearrange words to form a question. Just add か at the end:

> これは何ですか？ (Kore wa nan desu ka?) = What is this?
> どこに行きますか？ (Doko ni ikimasu ka?) = Where are you going?
> 田中さんは来ましたか？ (Tanaka-san wa kimashita ka?) = Did Tanaka-san come?

In casual speech, か is often dropped and replaced with a rising intonation:
> 行く？ (Iku?) = You going?

## て-form: The Swiss Army Knife

The て-form connects actions, makes requests, shows ongoing states, and appears in dozens of grammar patterns. It's the most versatile verb form:

| て-form use | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Connecting actions | 食べて寝た | Ate and slept |
| Request (polite) | 見てください | Please look |
| Progressive | 食べています | I am eating |
| Permission | 行ってもいい | You may go |
| Prohibition | 触ってはいけない | You must not touch |
| Try doing | 食べてみた | I tried eating it |
| Do in advance | 買っておく | Buy (it) in advance |
| Give/do for someone | 教えてあげる | I'll teach you (as a favor) |

## Counters — A Uniquely Japanese Challenge

Japanese uses specific "counter" words depending on what you're counting. There's no generic "things":

| Counter | For | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 個 (こ / ko) | small objects | りんご2個 (2 apples) |
| 枚 (まい / mai) | flat things | 紙3枚 (3 sheets of paper) |
| 本 (ほん / hon) | long, thin things | ペン1本 (1 pen), ビール2本 (2 bottles of beer) |
| 冊 (さつ / satsu) | bound books | 本5冊 (5 books) |
| 台 (だい / dai) | machines, vehicles | 車2台 (2 cars) |
| 匹 (ひき / hiki) | small animals | 猫3匹 (3 cats) |
| 頭 (とう / tō) | large animals | 牛2頭 (2 cows) |
| 羽 (わ / wa) | birds, rabbits | 鳥5羽 (5 birds) |
| 人 (にん / nin) | people | 3人 (3 people) — but: 1 person = 一人 (hitori), 2 = 二人 (futari) |
| 杯 (はい / hai) | cups, glasses, bowls | コーヒー1杯 (1 cup of coffee) |

## Sentence Patterns to Know at Every Level

### Beginner
- A は B です。— A is B.
- A が好きです。— I like A.
- A に行きます。— I'm going to A.
- A をください。— Please give me A.

### Intermediate
- A てから B。— After doing A, do B.
- A ので B。— Because of A, B. (reason)
- A たり B たりする。— Do things like A and B.
- A ようになった。— It came to be that A. (change over time)

### Advanced
- A に違いない。— It must certainly be A.
- A わけにはいかない。— I can't possibly do A (given the circumstances).
- A かねない。— There's a risk that A might happen.
- A にもかかわらず。— Despite A, nevertheless...

## Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Using の for all possession | 私の行く | 私が行く | の doesn't replace が as subject |
| Forgetting verb-final order | 私は飲む水を | 私は水を飲む | Verb always at end |
| は vs が confusion | 誰は来た？ | 誰が来た？ | Question words use が |
| Adjective past tense | 美しかったです ✓ | 美しいでした ✗ | い-adj conjugates before です |
| Direct translation of "have" | 私は弟があります | 私は弟がいます | あります is for things; います for living beings |

---

Grammar is a tool, not a cage. The more you read and listen to Japanese, the more these patterns will become intuitive. Don't aim to memorize rules — aim to recognize patterns.
