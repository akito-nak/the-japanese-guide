# Hiragana — Your First 46 Friends

> *"Hiragana is the gateway. Once you know it, Japan opens up."*

Welcome to the starting line. Hiragana is the foundational phonetic alphabet of Japanese — every sound the language makes can be written in hiragana. Most learners master it in **1–2 weeks** with daily practice. There are no shortcuts, but there are mnemonics, and we've got them.

---

## What Is Hiragana, Exactly?

Japanese has three writing systems (yes, three — breathe):

| System | Use | Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Hiragana** | Native Japanese words, grammar, verb endings | 46 base + variations |
| **Katakana** | Foreign loanwords, emphasis, science terms | 46 base + variations |
| **Kanji** | Meaning-based characters borrowed from Chinese | 2,000+ commonly used |

You'll use hiragana for **everything** at first. Most Japanese learning materials add small hiragana (called **furigana**) above kanji to help beginners read — so knowing hiragana unlocks a lot more than just hiragana words.

## The Full Hiragana Chart

Hiragana is organized by vowel columns and consonant rows. Learn the vowels first — they never change.

### The Five Vowels (and the heart of the system)

| Character | Reading | Sound like... |
|---|---|---|
| あ | a | "ah" (father) |
| い | i | "ee" (feet) |
| う | u | "oo" (but shorter, unrounded lips) |
| え | e | "eh" (bed) |
| お | o | "oh" (open) |

## Special Characters

### Small っ (Double Consonant)
A small っ before a consonant doubles that consonant — creating a brief pause or "stop":
- きって (kitte) = stamp
- ざっし (zasshi) = magazine
- ちょっと (chotto) = a little

### Long Vowels
In hiragana, long vowels are extended with another vowel character:
- おかあさん (okaasan) = mother — the aa is one long "ah" sound
- おにいさん (oniisan) = older brother
- おおきい (ookii) = big

## Stroke Order — The Complete Guide

Stroke order isn't just tradition — it creates the proportions, flow, and angles that make handwriting legible. Deviate from it and your characters look malformed, even to people who can't explain why. Learn it correctly from day one and it becomes automatic.

### The Seven Rules

| Rule | Description |
|---|---|
| **1. Top to bottom** | Upper strokes come before lower strokes |
| **2. Left to right** | Left strokes come before right strokes |
| **3. Horizontal before vertical** | When lines cross, draw the horizontal first (usually) |
| **4. Diagonal right-to-left first** | `ノ`-type diagonals before `\`-type diagonals |
| **5. Center before outer** | For symmetric characters, center stroke first |
| **6. Enclosure, then contents, then close** | Draw the frame, fill it, then seal the bottom |
| **7. Compound strokes don't lift** | A "stroke" is everything written without lifting the pen — including curves and direction changes |

Rule 7 trips up most beginners. In hiragana especially, one stroke can change direction multiple times. **Do not lift the pen mid-stroke.**

---

### How to Read the Stroke Diagrams

Each character entry shows:
- The character and its stroke count
- Strokes numbered **①②③** in order
- Direction arrows: `→` right `↓` down `↙` down-left `↘` down-right `↺` curve

A single stroke may contain multiple arrows — follow the number through its full path.

**Best practice:** watch an animated stroke order guide once, *then* write the character 10 times. Hand memory locks in faster when you observe first, then produce.

### The Vowels — Master These First

Every Japanese syllable ends in one of these five sounds. They are the foundation.

---

**あ (a) — 3 strokes**

```
  ① ─────
  ② ↙↩ → ↗
      ③ ↘
```

① Short horizontal stroke going right (upper area)  
② Start below ①, sweep down-left, loop back right, end with slight upward curve  
③ Short diagonal going down-right, crossing through the center of the loop  

---

**い (i) — 2 strokes**

```
  ①↙    ②↙
         ↗
```

① Left stroke: curves gently down and to the left  
② Right stroke: curves down, then hooks back up and to the right at the base  

---

**う (u) — 2 strokes**

```
    ① ─
  ②→ ↓ ↺
```

① Short near-horizontal mark at the very top (like a small accent)  
② Sweeps right, curves down, comes back up to the left — open shape, not fully closed  

---

**え (e) — 4 strokes**

```
  ① ────
    ②↓
  ③ ──
       ④ ↘↺
```

① Horizontal right (top)  
② Short vertical down through the center  
③ Shorter horizontal right (middle area, left of ②)  
④ Starts right-center, sweeps out right, curves down-left, loops back up  

---

**お (o) — 3 strokes**

```
  ① ────
  ② ↓
  ③ → ↙ ↺
```

① Horizontal right (top)  
② Vertical down (slight rightward curve at base)  
③ Horizontal right, then sweeps down-left, curves up forming a small hook

### K-Row (か行)

| Char | Count | Key notes |
|---|---|---|
| か (ka) | 3 | ①→ ②↓ (curves slightly right) ③↙ diagonal crossing ② |
| き (ki) | 4 | ①→ ②→ (both horizontal) ③↓↺ (vertical with curve) ④↙ detached diagonal |
| く (ku) | **1** | Start upper-right, sweep ↙, curve right — **one unbroken stroke** |
| け (ke) | 3 | ①↓ ②→ hooks ↓ ③↙ diagonal |
| こ (ko) | 2 | ①→ (top horizontal) ②→ sweeps down and curves back left |

> **く warning:** Most beginners write this as two strokes. It is one. Keep the pen down through the entire sweep.

---

### S-Row (さ行)

| Char | Count | Key notes |
|---|---|---|
| さ (sa) | 3 | ①→ ②→ ③↓ sweeps right then curves up-right |
| し (shi) | **1** | Start top-center, sweep ↓, curve strongly right at the bottom — one stroke |
| す (su) | 2 | ①→↓ (cross shape) ②→ curves around into a downward loop |
| せ (se) | 3 | ①↓ ②→ ③↓ with outward curve at base |
| そ (so) | 2 | ①→ ②S-like sweep: right, down-left, curves right |

> **し tip:** The bottom hook is part of the same stroke as the downward sweep. Practice in one fluid motion.

---

### T-Row (た行)

| Char | Count | Key notes |
|---|---|---|
| た (ta) | 4 | ①→ ②↓ ③→ hooks ↓ ④↙ diagonal |
| ち (chi) | 2 | ①→ curves down ②larger curve ↓ loops right at bottom |
| つ (tsu) | **1** | Sweep right → curve steeply ↓ → curve back left — one stroke |
| て (te) | **1** | →, dips ↓, curves ↗ — one flowing stroke |
| と (to) | 2 | ①↓ short vertical ②→ curves ↙, hooks back ↗ |

> **て and つ are single strokes** despite appearing complex. Write each without lifting the pen.

---

### N-Row (な行)

| Char | Count | Key notes |
|---|---|---|
| な (na) | 4 | ①→ ②↓ ③→ ④complex: ↙→↺ (loop) |
| に (ni) | 3 | ①→ ②↓ ③└ shape: → then ↓ with slight hook |
| ぬ (nu) | 2 | ①↓ curves right ②→↙↺ with a loop at bottom |
| ね (ne) | 2 | ①↓ ②→↙↺ large loop (similar to れ) |
| の (no) | **1** | One clockwise oval: start upper-left, sweep → ↓ ← ↑, do not close |

> **の is a single oval stroke** — continuous, never lifted, not fully closed at the top.

---

### H-Row (は行)

| Char | Count | Key notes |
|---|---|---|
| は (ha) | 3 | ①↓ ②→↓ ③→↙↺ (loop, like a lasso going left) |
| ひ (hi) | 2 | ①↙ ②→ curves into a closed loop |
| ふ (fu) | 4 | ①→ ②↙ ③↙ ④↺ small loop at bottom |
| へ (he) | **1** | Simple: ↗ then ↘ — an inverted V |
| ほ (ho) | 4 | Like は + additional loop: ①↓ ②→↓ ③→ ④↙↺ |

> **へ is the simplest hiragana** — one stroke, identical to the katakana ヘ.

---

### M-Row (ま行)

| Char | Count | Key notes |
|---|---|---|
| ま (ma) | 3 | ①→ ②→↓ ③↙↺ loop |
| み (mi) | 2 | ①↙ hook up ②→↙↺ complex with loop |
| む (mu) | 3 | ①→↓↺ ②→ ③↘ |
| め (me) | 2 | ①↙↩ ②→↙↺ |
| も (mo) | 3 | ①→ ②→ (two short horizontals) ③↓↺ |

---

### Y-Row, R-Row, W-Row, and ん

| Char | Count | Key notes |
|---|---|---|
| や (ya) | 3 | ①↙ ②→↓↺ ③→ |
| ゆ (yu) | 2 | ①↙↺ ②→↓ |
| よ (yo) | 2 | ①→ ②→↓← curves left at base |
| ら (ra) | 2 | ①→↓ ②→↙↺ |
| り (ri) | 2 | ①↙ ②↓ hooks right at base |
| る (ru) | **1** | Sweep →, curve ↓, loop, end with small rightward hook |
| れ (re) | 2 | ①↓ ②→↙↺ large loop |
| ろ (ro) | **1** | Like る but without the final hook — ends going left |
| わ (wa) | 2 | ①↓ ②→↙↺ |
| を (wo) | 3 | ①→ ②→↓ ③→↙↺ (rare in modern text) |
| ん (n) | **1** | Start upper-right, curve ↙, swing right and ↑ — ends with a flourish |

> **ん** catches everyone: the final flourish goes *up and to the right* — don't end it going down.

## Practice Strategy

### Week 1: Drill Method
- Day 1–2: Vowels + k-row (あいうえお、かきくけこ)
- Day 3–4: s-row + t-row (さしすせそ、たちつてと)
- Day 5–6: n-row + h-row (なにぬねの、はひふへほ)
- Day 7: m-row + y-row + r-row + w-row + ん

### Week 2: Reading Practice
Once you know the characters, **stop using romaji immediately.** Cold turkey. It's uncomfortable for about a week and then everything clicks.

Try reading:
- Hiragana-only children's books (*絵本* — ehon)
- NHK Web Easy (they have furigana on everything)
- Kids' anime with Japanese subtitles
