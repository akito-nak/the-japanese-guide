# Cities & Regions — Japan Beyond Tokyo

> *"Tokyo is extraordinary — but it's not all of Japan. Every city has its own dialect, its own food, its own pace, and its own personality. The more cities you see, the more you understand the country."*

Japan is divided into 47 prefectures (都道府県 — todōfuken) across four main islands (Honshū, Hokkaidō, Shikoku, Kyūshū) and Okinawa. Here's a guide to the major cities and regions, with the language and cultural notes you need for each.

---

## 東京 (Tōkyō) — The Capital

**Population:** ~14 million (metro: ~37 million — largest in the world)
**Dialect:** Standard Japanese / Tokyo dialect (東京弁)
**Vibe:** Intense, efficient, endlessly varied, expensive

Tokyo isn't one city — it's 23 special wards (特別区 — tokubetsuku) plus countless adjacent cities, each with distinct personality:

| Area | Character |
|---|---|
| 新宿 (Shinjuku) | Skyscrapers, Golden Gai, Kabukicho red-light district, transport hub |
| 渋谷 (Shibuya) | Youth fashion, Shibuya Crossing, music venues |
| 原宿 (Harajuku) | Street fashion, Takeshita Street, Omotesando luxury |
| 秋葉原 (Akihabara) | Electronics, anime goods, manga, maid cafes |
| 浅草 (Asakusa) | Old Tokyo (下町 — shitamachi), Senso-ji temple, rickshaws |
| 銀座 (Ginza) | High-end shopping, galleries, cocktail bars |
| 六本木 (Roppongi) | Nightlife, international crowd, art museums |
| 上野 (Ueno) | Museums, zoo, Ameyoko market, Ueno Park |
| 下北沢 (Shimokitazawa) | Vintage shops, live music, indie cafes |
| 吉祥寺 (Kichijoji) | Inokashira Park, consistently voted "best place to live in Tokyo" |

**Language note:** Tokyo Japanese sets the standard, but real Tokyo casual speech drops particles heavily and uses distinct local vocabulary. You'll hear **じゃん (jan)** constantly — Tokyo's equivalent of "right?" / "isn't it?"

## 大阪 (Ōsaka) — The Kitchen of Japan

**Population:** ~2.7 million (metro: ~19 million)
**Dialect:** Osaka-ben (関西弁 core)
**Vibe:** Loud, warm, food-obsessed, funny

Osaka has a distinct identity from Tokyo — more direct, more comedic, more focused on food and fun. The city of merchants and comedians.

| Area | Character |
|---|---|
| 道頓堀 (Dōtonbori) | Neon-lit food/entertainment district, Glico running man sign |
| なんば (Namba) | Shopping, nightlife, comedy theaters, takoyaki |
| 梅田 (Umeda) | Osaka's Shinjuku — major transport hub, shopping |
| 心斎橋 (Shinsaibashi) | Shopping street, fashion |
| 天王寺 (Tennōji) | Zoo, Abeno Harukas (tallest building), working-class vibe |
| 新世界 (Shinsekai) | Old-fashioned entertainment district, Tsutenkaku tower, kushikatsu |

**Osaka phrases:**
| Standard | Osaka | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| すごい | めっちゃ | Very |
| 本当に | ほんまに | Really |
| 美味しい | うまい / まいう | Delicious |
| ありがとう | おおきに | Thank you |
| ダメ | あかん | No good |
| 良い | ええ | Good |

**Osaka culture:** The city proverb is **くいだおれ (kuidaore)** — "eating yourself bankrupt." Osakans are known for spending lavishly on food and being blunt about business. The comedy tradition (吉本興業 — Yoshimoto Kogyo) is based here.

## 京都 (Kyōto) — The Ancient Capital

**Population:** ~1.4 million
**Dialect:** Kyoto-ben (soft, classical Kansai)
**Vibe:** Refined, traditional, deeply historical, expensive

Japan's capital for over 1,000 years (794–1868). More temples and shrines per square kilometer than almost anywhere on earth. The spiritual and aesthetic heart of traditional Japan.

| Area | Character |
|---|---|
| 嵐山 (Arashiyama) | Bamboo grove, river, temples, kimono rentals |
| 祇園 (Gion) | Geisha district, tea houses, cobblestone streets |
| 清水寺 (Kiyomizudera) | Famous hilltop temple with wooden stage |
| 伏見稲荷 (Fushimi Inari) | Thousands of torii gates up a mountain — iconic |
| 金閣寺 (Kinkakuji) | Gold Pavilion |
| 哲学の道 (Tetsugaku no Michi) | Philosopher's Path — cherry blossom-lined canal |
| 錦市場 (Nishiki Ichiba) | Narrow covered market — "Kyoto's kitchen" |

**Kyoto culture notes:**
- **一見さんお断り (Ichigen-san okotowari):** Some traditional establishments in Gion don't accept first-time customers without an introduction. This is old merchant culture.
- **Geisha vs. Maiko:** Geisha (芸者/芸妓) are fully trained. Maiko (舞妓) are apprentices. Photographing them without permission is considered rude — Gion has had to put up "no photography" signs.
- Kyoto dialect is softer and more polite-sounding than Osaka dialect — centuries of court proximity.

## 神戸 (Kōbe) — The Cosmopolitan Port

**Population:** ~1.5 million
**Dialect:** Kansai-ben (milder than Osaka)
**Vibe:** Stylish, international, comfortable

Japan's gateway to the world since 1858. Kobe has large Chinese and Western historical presence, resulting in a distinctive cosmopolitan character.

- **南京町 (Nankingai):** Kobe's Chinatown — Japan's most authentic
- **北野異人館 (Kitano Ijinkan):** Western-style houses from the foreign settlement era
- **神戸牛 (Kōbe Gyū):** Kobe beef — some of the world's most expensive meat. Raised in the Tajima region.
- **ルミナリエ (Luminarie):** Annual December light installation commemorating the 1995 Great Hanshin Earthquake

## 名古屋 (Nagoya) — The Practical City

**Population:** ~2.3 million
**Dialect:** Nagoya-ben
**Vibe:** Industrial, practical, understated, strong local identity

Japan's fourth-largest city, home to Toyota HQ. Often overlooked by tourists — which means it's less crowded and more affordable.

**Nagoya food (名古屋めし — Nagoya meshi):**
| Food | Description |
|---|---|
| 味噌カツ (miso katsu) | Pork cutlet with rich miso sauce |
| 手羽先 (tebasa) | Chicken wings — sweet soy and pepper |
| 名古屋コーチン (Nagoya Kochin) | Local chicken breed — superior quality |
| あんかけスパ (ankake spa) | Thick sauce spaghetti — only in Nagoya |
| ひつまぶし (hitsumabushi) | Grilled eel over rice, eaten three ways |
| 小倉トースト (ogura toast) | Toast with red bean paste and butter |

## 広島 (Hiroshima) — Resilience and Peace

**Population:** ~1.2 million
**Dialect:** Hiroshima-ben
**Vibe:** Warm, resilient, surprisingly vibrant

Hiroshima is an extraordinary city — rebuilt from nuclear destruction in 1945 into a thriving modern city that now advocates powerfully for peace.

**Essential visits:**
- **平和記念公園 (Heiwa Kinen Kōen):** Peace Memorial Park and Museum — deeply moving
- **原爆ドーム (Genbaku Dōmu):** Atomic Bomb Dome — only surviving structure near the hypocenter
- **宮島 (Miyajima):** Nearby island with the famous floating torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine

**Hiroshima food:**
- お好み焼き (okonomiyaki) — Hiroshima style is layered (noodles inside), not mixed
- 牡蠣 (kaki) — Hiroshima produces ~60% of Japan's oysters
- もみじまんじゅう (momiji manjū) — maple leaf-shaped sweet filled with red bean paste

## 福岡 (Fukuoka) — Gateway to Asia

**Population:** ~1.6 million
**Dialect:** Hakata-ben (博多弁)
**Vibe:** Energetic, youthful, warm, growing fast

Japan's fastest-growing major city. Closest major city to China and Korea — the cultural crossover shows in the food and atmosphere. The city is divided into two areas: Fukuoka (newer, western) and Hakata (older, eastern, where the station is).

**Fukuoka food:**
| Food | Description |
|---|---|
| 博多ラーメン (Hakata ramen) | Tonkotsu broth, thin noodles, 替え玉 (extra noodles) |
| 明太子 (mentaiko) | Spiced cod roe — Fukuoka's most famous export |
| もつ鍋 (motsu nabe) | Offal hot pot — rich, deeply flavored |
| 水炊き (mizutaki) | Chicken hot pot with ponzu |
| 屋台 (yatai) | Fukuoka has Japan's most famous yatai stalls — open-air food stalls at night along the river |

## 札幌 (Sapporo) — Hokkaido's Capital

**Population:** ~1.96 million
**Dialect:** Hokkaido dialect (closest to standard Japanese)
**Vibe:** Cold, open, outdoorsy, dairy-obsessed

Hokkaido is Japan's northernmost and newest major island — only settled from the 1870s (the indigenous Ainu people have lived there for thousands of years). Hokkaido has wide roads, open landscapes, and a different feel from the rest of Japan.

**Sapporo food (Hokkaido食文化):**
| Food | Description |
|---|---|
| 味噌ラーメン (miso ramen) | Sapporo's contribution to ramen culture |
| ジンギスカン (Genghis Khan / Jingisukan) | Lamb BBQ — barbecue on a dome grill |
| カニ (kani) | Crab — especially 毛ガニ (hairy crab) and タラバ (king crab) |
| スープカレー (soup curry) | Sapporo's unique curry style — thin, spiced broth |
| 乳製品 (nyūseihin) | Dairy — Hokkaido milk, butter, cheese, soft-serve ice cream |
| じゃがいも (jagaimo) | Potatoes — Hokkaido grows Japan's best |

**Sapporo Snow Festival (さっぽろ雪まつり):** February event with enormous ice and snow sculptures — one of Japan's great winter events.

## 沖縄 (Okinawa) — The Tropical Islands

**Population:** ~1.47 million
**Language:** Okinawan Japanese / Uchinaaguchi
**Vibe:** Relaxed, warm, culturally distinct, subtropical

Okinawa is its own cultural world — historically the independent Ryūkyū Kingdom (琉球王国) until the late 19th century. The climate, food, music, and people have a different character from mainland Japan.

| Area | Character |
|---|---|
| 那覇 (Naha) | Capital — Shuri Castle, Kokusai-dōri shopping street |
| 美ら海水族館 (Churaumi Aquarium) | World-class aquarium with whale sharks |
| 古宇利島 (Kouri Island) | Beautiful bridge, beaches |
| 竹富島 (Taketomi Island) | Traditional white-walled village |

**Okinawan food:**
| Food | Description |
|---|---|
| ゴーヤーチャンプルー | Bitter melon stir-fry — Okinawa's most iconic dish |
| ソーキそば | Okinawan noodles with pork ribs |
| ラフテー (rafute) | Slow-braised pork belly |
| ヒラヤーチー | Thin savory pancake |
| 泡盛 (awamori) | Distilled Okinawan spirit — made from Thai-style rice |

**Okinawa and longevity:** Okinawa has long had one of the world's highest concentrations of centenarians (百寿者 — hyakujusha). This has been attributed to diet (lots of vegetables, tofu, and small portions of pork), community, sense of purpose (生き甲斐 — ikigai), and slower pace of life.

## Regional Language Quick Reference

| Region | Key phrase | Standard | Dialect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Osaka | "Isn't it?" | でしょ | やん / ちゃう |
| Fukuoka | "Right?" | ですよ | ばい |
| Nagoya | "That's right" | そうですね | そうだがや |
| Hiroshima | Request | 〜してください | 〜しんさい |
| Kyoto | "Thank you" | ありがとう | おおきに (formal contexts) |
| Okinawa | "Hello" | こんにちは | はいさい / はいたい |

| Region | Prefectures | Key cities |
|---|---|---|
| 北海道 (Hokkaidō) | Hokkaido | Sapporo, Hakodate |

| 四国 (Shikoku) | Ehime, Kagawa, Kochi, Tokushima | Matsuyama, Takamatsu |
| 九州 (Kyūshū) | Fukuoka, Nagasaki, Kumamoto, Oita, etc. | Fukuoka, Nagasaki |
