# JLPT N2 — Near Fluent

> *"N2 is where Japanese becomes useful in the real world. It's required for many university programs in Japan, preferred or required for many professional positions, and the level where you can watch the news and mostly understand it."*

N2 represents upper-intermediate proficiency. You can understand Japanese used in a wide variety of situations — not just everyday casual contexts, but also moderately complex media, professional situations, and extended written texts. This is a genuinely significant milestone.

---

## What N2 Covers

| Area | Target |
|---|---|
| Vocabulary | ~6,000 words |
| Kanji | ~1,000 characters |
| Grammar | ~200+ patterns (more complex) |
| Reading | Newspaper articles, formal letters, complex explanations |
| Listening | Near-natural speed, news broadcasts, complex conversations |

The vocabulary jump from N3 (~3,750) to N2 (~6,000) is large — over 2,000 new words, many of them abstract, technical, or classical in origin.

## N2 Grammar — Selected Key Patterns

### Formal Written Expressions
| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜において/〜における | At / in (formal location) | 会議において決定された。|
| 〜に関して/〜に関する | Regarding / concerning | この問題に関して意見がある。|
| 〜に対して/〜に対する | Toward / against | 批判に対して反論した。|
| 〜を通じて/〜を通して | Through / via | 交渉を通じて解決した。|
| 〜として | As / in the role of | 代表として参加する。|
| 〜にわたって | Over a period of / throughout | 3年にわたって研究した。|
| 〜をはじめ(として) | Starting with / including | 東京をはじめ各地で。|
| 〜に伴って | Along with / as ~ happens | 高齢化に伴って問題が増える。|

### Expressing Degree and Limit
| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜にすぎない | Nothing more than / merely | 噂にすぎない。|
| 〜にほかならない | Is nothing other than | これは詐欺にほかならない。|
| 〜に過ぎる | Too ~ | 難しすぎる。|
| 〜こそ | Precisely / exactly (emphasis) | 今こそチャンスだ。|
| 〜さえ | Even / even if | 子供でさえ知っている。|
| 〜ほど〜ない | Not as ~ as | 思ったほど難しくない。|
| 〜に至っては | When it comes to (extreme case) | 彼に至っては全く知らない。|

### Expressing Stance and Opinion
| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜に違いない | Must be (strong certainty) | 彼が犯人に違いない。|
| 〜はずがない | Cannot possibly be | そんなはずがない。|
| 〜わけがない | There's no reason that ~ | できるわけがない。|
| 〜わけにはいかない | Can't afford to / must not | 諦めるわけにはいかない。|
| 〜ものだ | Naturally is / used to (nostalgic) | 昔はよく遊んだものだ。|
| 〜ものではない | Is not something that should be | 嘘をつくものではない。|

### Concessive and Adversative
| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜ものの | Although / even though | 行ったものの何もできなかった。|
| 〜にもかかわらず | Despite / regardless of | 反対にもかかわらず実行した。|
| 〜とはいえ | Even so / having said that | 忙しいとはいえ連絡できる。|
| 〜ながら(も) | While ~ / even though | 知りながら黙っていた。|
| 〜からには | Since / now that | 決めたからには頑張る。|

## Reading at N2

N2 reading tests your ability to:
- Understand newspaper and magazine articles
- Follow editorial opinions and arguments
- Read formal notices, official letters
- Understand long-form explanations (600–1,000 characters)
- Identify implicit meaning and writer's intent

**Reading strategies for N2:**
1. Build speed — N2 passages are longer, time is tight
2. Practice identifying paragraph structure (main claim → support → conclusion)
3. Study how Japanese writers signal concession (〜ものの, 〜とはいえ)
4. Practice with real newspaper editorials (朝日新聞 社説)

---

## Listening at N2

N2 listening includes:
- News-style announcements at near-broadcast speed
- Complex conversations between multiple speakers
- Lectures and extended monologues
- Passages where you must infer the speaker's attitude or intent

**Key skill:** Not just comprehension, but inference. "What does this person actually think?" when they're being polite about disagreeing.

## 16-Week N2 Study Plan

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Vocabulary | 1–4 | 2,000+ new words ([Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net) + [So-matome N2](https://www.ask-books.com/somatome/)) |
| Grammar | 5–8 | [新完全マスター N2 文法](https://www.ask-books.com/shinkanzen/) (all patterns) |
| Kanji | 9–10 | ~350 new kanji + compounds |
| Reading | 11–12 | Newspaper articles, [NHK](https://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/easy/), practice passages |
| Listening | 13–14 | [NHK radio](https://www.nhk.or.jp/radio/), JLPT audio, drama without subtitles |
| Practice Tests | 15–16 | 3+ full practice tests, targeted review |

**Daily commitment:** 2+ hours. N2 is not part-time — treat it like a course.

## What N2 Opens

**Career:**
- Many Japanese companies list N2 as a hiring requirement for non-Japanese applicants
- Graduate school applications in Japan often require N2
- Professional visa applications may accept N2

**Real-world ability:**
- Read most newspaper articles with a dictionary
- Watch news broadcasts and understand ~80%
- Read most manga without furigana
- Participate in most workplace conversations
- Write professional emails and reports

**Immersion:** At N2 level, you can consume most Japanese media without subtitles and extract enough meaning to enjoy it. This is the point where immersion truly accelerates.
