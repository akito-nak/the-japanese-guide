# JLPT N1 — The Summit

> *"N1 is not the end of Japanese learning — it's the point where you finally have enough tools to learn everything else from the language itself. Most N1 holders will tell you they learned more Japanese after N1 than before."*

N1 represents advanced proficiency — the ability to understand Japanese at the level needed for most professional, academic, and social contexts. Only a fraction of non-native learners reach N1. Those who do can genuinely participate in Japanese society at a sophisticated level.

---

## What N1 Covers

| Area | Target |
|---|---|
| Vocabulary | ~10,000+ words |
| Kanji | ~2,000 characters (full Jōyō list + beyond) |
| Grammar | Complex, classical-influenced, nuanced patterns |
| Reading | Essays, editorials, literary texts, legal/technical documents |
| Listening | Full-speed natural speech, complex discussion, subtle nuance |

The vocabulary at N1 includes:
- Classical Japanese vocabulary surviving in formal writing
- Technical vocabulary across multiple domains
- Literary and archaic expressions
- Subtle distinctions between near-synonyms

## N1 Grammar — Advanced Patterns

N1 grammar is dense with formal, written, and classical-influenced patterns:

### Classical and Literary Forms
| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜ごとし/ごとく | Like ~ / as if ~ (classical) | 嵐のごとく突撃した。|
| 〜べく | In order to / should (classical て-form) | 解決すべく努力する。|
| 〜たる | Being ~ / as (formal attributive copula) | 国家たる責任がある。|
| 〜しかるべき | As appropriate / proper | しかるべき対応をとる。|
| 〜にして | While being / as well as | 天才にして努力家でもある。|
| 〜いかん | Depending on / according to | 結果いかんでは再考する。|
| 〜といわず〜といわず | Not just ~ but also ~ | 昼といわず夜といわず働く。|

### Expressing Conditions and Nuance
| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜をよそに | Heedless of / indifferent to | 周囲の心配をよそに出発した。|
| 〜もさることながら | Not only ~ but also | 技術もさることながら経験も必要だ。|
| 〜に至るまで | To the extent of / up to | 細部に至るまで確認した。|
| 〜ならではの | Unique to / only possible with | 京都ならではの景色。|
| 〜にたえる | Worthy of / can bear/endure | 読むにたえる文章。|
| 〜に即して | In accordance with / in line with | 実態に即して考える。|
| 〜をもって | By means of / with | 実力をもって示した。|

### Concession and Contrast (Advanced)
| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜ではあるまいか | Might it not be that / I wonder if | 誤りではあるまいか。|
| 〜ずにはいられない | Can't help but do | 笑わずにはいられない。|
| 〜を余儀なくされる | Be forced to / have no choice but | 中止を余儀なくされた。|
| 〜ならいざしらず | I don't know about ~ but... | 昔ならいざしらず今は違う。|
| 〜はさておき | Setting ~ aside | 冗談はさておき。|

## N1 Vocabulary: What's Different

At N1, vocabulary is less about quantity and more about nuance:

### Near-Synonyms That Must Be Distinguished
Japanese has many near-synonyms with subtle usage differences:

| Pair | Distinction |
|---|---|
| 見る / 見える / 目にする | Active looking / passive seeing / happen to see |
| 思う / 考える / 信じる | Casual opinion / deliberate reasoning / believe firmly |
| 言う / 述べる / 語る | Say casually / state formally / narrate/tell |
| 変わる / 変化する / 変容する | Change (general) / change (scientific/formal) / transform |
| 行う / 実施する / 遂行する | Carry out (general) / implement / execute/accomplish |
| 始まる / 開始する / 着手する | Begin (general) / begin (formal) / start work on |

### Classical and Literary Vocabulary
| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 由来 | yurai | Origin / derivation |
| 趣 | omomuki | Taste / atmosphere / gist |
| 様相 | yōsō | Aspect / appearance |
| 所以 | yuen | Reason / cause (literary) |
| 齟齬 | sogo | Discord / mismatch |
| 辻褄 | tsujitsuma | Consistency (辻褄が合う = consistent) |
| 見識 | kenshiki | Discernment / insight |
| 蔑ろ | naigashiro | Disregard / neglect |
| 弊害 | heigai | Harmful effects / evil |
| 妥当 | datō | Appropriate / valid |

### 四字熟語 (Yojijukugo) at N1
N1 tests yojijukugo (four-character idioms) in depth:

| Idiom | Meaning |
|---|---|

| 一石二鳥 (isseki nichō) | Kill two birds with one stone |

## N1 Reading

N1 reading includes:
- Editorial columns and opinion essays (800–1,000 characters)
- Literary excerpts with classical language features
- Technical articles from specific fields
- Multiple connected passages (comparing different texts on the same topic)
- Inference and critical reading questions

**What makes N1 reading hard:**
1. Speed: passages are long, time is short
2. Subtlety: answer choices are close; you must find the precise best answer
3. Classical grammar: some passages use pre-modern language features
4. Inference: the correct answer is often not directly stated

**N1 Reading Strategy:**
- Read widely in Japanese literature and editorials from months before the exam
- Practice reading at 500+ characters per minute
- Study classical grammar features (〜べし, 〜ず, 〜ごとし) separately
- For comparison passages (comparing two or more texts), map out author positions before answering

---

## N1 Listening

N1 listening tests:
- Extended monologues (lectures, talks, explanations)
- Nuanced conversation where speaker intent must be inferred
- Multiple-choice questions requiring comparison of multiple statements
- Integrated understanding (combine information from different parts of audio)

The difficulty: speakers at N1 level speak naturally, with all the contractions, hedging, and implied meaning of real Japanese conversation. You must understand both what is said and what is meant.

## Study Plan for N1

N1 preparation is typically **6–12 months** from N2 level, depending on your reading and listening habits.

### Phase 1 (Months 1–3): Build the vocabulary base
- Learn remaining kanji to full Jōyō + 200 extras
- [新完全マスター N1 語彙](https://www.ask-books.com/shinkanzen/) (vocabulary)
- [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net) mining from reading

### Phase 2 (Months 3–5): Grammar and expression
- [新完全マスター N1 文法](https://www.ask-books.com/shinkanzen/)
- 新完全マスター N1 表現 (expressions — yojijukugo, idioms)
- Classical Japanese grammar basics (classical verb forms)

### Phase 3 (Months 5–8): Immersion-intensive reading
- Read 1–2 newspaper articles daily ([朝日新聞](https://www.asahi.com)、[読売新聞](https://www.yomiuri.co.jp)、[毎日新聞](https://mainichi.jp) 社説)
- Read short stories and literary prose
- Read technical articles in your field
- Mine vocabulary from everything

### Phase 4 (Months 8–10): Listening intensive
- [NHK news radio](https://www.nhk.or.jp/radio/)
- Japanese political discussion programs
- Drama without subtitles (complex plots, varied register)
- JLPT N1 practice listening tracks

### Phase 5 (Months 10–12): Test preparation
- 3+ full official-level practice tests
- Detailed error analysis
- Targeted vocabulary and grammar review
- Timed reading practice

## The N1 Mindset

N1 isn't won in the last few months of cramming. It's built over years:

**Years 1–2:** N5 → N3 foundation
**Years 2–3:** N3 → N2 breadth
**Years 3–5:** N2 → N1 depth

Successful N1 candidates almost universally describe the same key habit: **reading and listening to Japanese every single day**, not just studying it. The vocabulary and nuance required at N1 is too large to memorize — it must be absorbed through sustained exposure.

## After N1: What Comes Next?

N1 holders often describe the same experience: **Japanese becomes a tool, not a subject.** You stop thinking about Japanese and start thinking in it, with it, through it.

What N1 opens:
- Most Japanese university and graduate school admissions
- Professional positions requiring Japanese literacy
- Legal, medical, and academic translation work
- Full enjoyment of Japanese literature, film, and media without subtitles or aids
- The ability to continue learning Japanese entirely *from* Japanese

What remains after N1:
- **地方語 (dialect)** — You still may struggle in Osaka or Tohoku
- **文語 (classical Japanese)** — University-level study is a separate discipline
- **専門語 (technical vocabulary)** — Law, medicine, engineering each have their own deep vocabulary
- **生きた日本語 (living Japanese)** — Slang, generational expression, internet language — these never stop evolving

N1 is not the end. It is the beginning of reading the room.
