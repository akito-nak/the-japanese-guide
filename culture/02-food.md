# Japanese Food — Eating Your Way Through the Language

> *"Japan has more Michelin stars than any other country. It also has the world's greatest convenience store food. Both things are true. Both are wonderful."*

Food is one of the fastest ways into Japanese culture — and one of the most delicious reasons to learn the language. Menus, ingredient names, restaurant phrases, regional specialties — it's all accessible and immediately rewarding to study.

---

## The Philosophy of Japanese Food

Japanese cuisine (和食 — washoku) was designated UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2013. Its defining qualities:

- **Umami (旨味):** The "fifth taste" — savory, deep flavor from dashi, soy sauce, miso, fermented ingredients. The concept was identified and named by Japanese chemist Kikunae Ikeda in 1908.
- **Seasonality (旬 — shun):** Ingredients are used at their peak season. Eating out-of-season ingredients is aesthetically wrong, not just culinarily.
- **Presentation (盛り付け — moritsuke):** How food looks is part of how it tastes. Japanese plating is a craft.
- **Balance (五味 — gomi):** The five tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami) should be represented.
- **Freshness:** Especially for raw fish. Japan's fish markets (like Tsukiji/Toyosu) operate on a different plane.

## The Big Dishes

### Sushi (寿司)
Not just raw fish on rice. The world:

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| 握り寿司 (nigirizushi) | Hand-pressed fish on seasoned rice |
| 巻き寿司 (makizushi) | Rolls — inside-out, maki, etc. |
| ちらし寿司 (chirashizushi) | Scattered toppings over a bowl of rice |
| ちゃんこ寿司 | Regional variations |
| 押し寿司 (oshizushi) | Pressed sushi in a mold (Osaka specialty) |

**At a sushi restaurant:**
> 〜をひとつください。 — One ~, please.
> おまかせでお願いします。 — Chef's choice (omakase — "I leave it to you")
> わさびぬきでお願いします。 — Without wasabi, please.
> 醤油はどこですか？ — Where's the soy sauce?

### Ramen (ラーメン)
Ramen is a regional religion in Japan. The four main bases:

| Style | Region | Broth | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|
| 醤油 (shōyu) | Tokyo | Soy sauce | Clear to brown, classic |
| 塩 (shio) | Hokkaido | Salt | Clear, delicate |
| 味噌 (miso) | Sapporo | Miso paste | Rich, hearty |
| 豚骨 (tonkotsu) | Fukuoka/Hakata | Pork bone | Opaque, rich, fatty |

Ordering ramen:
> 硬めでお願いします。 — Firm noodles, please.
> 替え玉ください。 — Another portion of noodles, please. (Hakata specialty)
> からめでお願いします。 — Extra rich seasoning, please.

### Izakaya Food (居酒屋)
The izakaya — Japanese pub — is one of Japan's greatest inventions. Shared small plates, drinks, and hours:

| Dish | Romaji | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 枝豆 | edamame | Salted boiled soybeans |
| 唐揚げ | karaage | Japanese fried chicken |
| 焼き鳥 | yakitori | Grilled chicken skewers |
| たこ焼き | takoyaki | Octopus balls (Osaka) |
| お好み焼き | okonomiyaki | Savory pancake |
| 刺身盛り | sashimi mori | Sashimi assortment |
| 冷やっこ | hiyayakko | Cold tofu with toppings |
| 漬物 | tsukemono | Pickled vegetables |
| 締めのご飯 | shime no gohan | The final carb course at the end |

Izakaya vocabulary:
> 生中ください！ — One draft beer (medium), please!
> とりあえずビールで。 — Beer to start, for now.
> おつまみ何かありますか？ — Do you have any bar snacks?
> お通しは何ですか？ — What's the cover charge dish? (お通し is a small appetizer you're charged for automatically)

## Regional Food Culture

Japan's regional food differences are serious business. Locals feel strongly about their local specialties:

| Region | Famous food |
|---|---|
| Hokkaido | Seafood, dairy (butter, milk, cheese), soup curry, miso ramen |
| Tohoku | Wanko soba (small bowls of soba served continuously), kiritanpo (rice sticks) |
| Tokyo | Monjayaki, Tokyo ramen (shōyu), Edomae sushi |
| Osaka | Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers), udon |
| Kyoto | Kaiseki (multi-course formal cuisine), yudōfu (tofu hot pot), Kyoto-style tsukemono |
| Hiroshima | Okonomiyaki (different from Osaka — layered, not mixed), oysters |
| Fukuoka | Hakata ramen (tonkotsu), mentaiko (spiced cod roe), motsu nabe |
| Okinawa | Champuru (stir-fry), Okinawa soba, goya (bitter melon), Awamori (spirit) |

## The Convenience Store Revolution

Japanese convenience stores (コンビニ — konbini) are a phenomenon. 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, and Lawson operate at a standard that would be upscale in other countries.

**What you can get at a konbini:**
- Hot steamed nikuman (meat buns)
- Onigiri (rice balls) in 20+ varieties
- Fresh sushi and sashimi
- Hot coffee
- Egg salad sandwiches (surprisingly incredible)
- Oden (hot pot in heated display case)
- Printouts, tax payments, event tickets, cash withdrawals
- Alcohol (until midnight in most prefectures)

**Konbini vocabulary:**
> 温めますか？ — Shall I heat this up? (Staff will ask about prepared foods)
> 袋はよろしいですか？ — Do you need a bag?
> ポイントカードはお持ちですか？ — Do you have a points card?

## Restaurant Phrases in Full

### Arriving
> いらっしゃいませ！ (staff) — Welcome!
> 〜名です。 — (Number) people. [Hold up fingers]
> 禁煙席をお願いします。 — Non-smoking section, please.
> 予約しています。田中です。 — I have a reservation. Name is Tanaka.

### Ordering
> 注文してもいいですか？ — May I order?
> これをください。 — I'll have this. [Point at menu]
> おすすめは何ですか？ — What do you recommend?
> 〜抜きでお願いします。 — Without ~, please.
> 〜アレルギーがあります。 — I have a ~ allergy.
> ベジタリアンです。 — I am vegetarian.

### During and After
> 美味しいです！ — It's delicious!
> お会計をお願いします。 — Check, please.
> 全部一緒でいいです。 — All together is fine. (One check)
> 別々でお願いします。 — Separate checks, please.
> ごちそうさまでした。 — Thank you for the meal.

## Food Taboos and Restrictions

Japan is not always easy for special diets, but awareness is growing:

**Vegetarians/Vegans:** Fish-based dashi (出汁) is in almost everything. Ask:
> 出汁はどんなものを使っていますか？ — What kind of dashi do you use?
> 魚介類は入っていますか？ — Does this contain seafood?

**Gluten-free:** Very difficult in Japan. Soy sauce contains wheat. Ask:
> グルテンフリーのものはありますか？ — Do you have gluten-free options?

**Halal:** Growing availability in tourist areas, especially Tokyo and Osaka. Look for ハラール (harāru) certification.

## Drinking Culture

**お酒 (osake):** Alcohol in general. The naming hierarchy:
- ビール (bīru) — beer: usually まず生ビール (draft) first
- 日本酒 (nihonshu) — sake: served hot (熱燗 — atsukan) or cold (冷 — hiya)
- 焼酎 (shōchū) — distilled spirit from sweet potato, barley, or rice
- ハイボール (haibōru) — whisky soda, massively popular
- チューハイ (chūhai) — fruit soda + shōchū, accessible and light
- ウイスキー (uisukī) — Japanese whisky: Suntory, Nikka — world-class

**Non-alcohol:**
- お茶 (ocha) — green tea: served free at many restaurants
- 麦茶 (mugicha) — barley tea: especially cold in summer
- 水 (mizu) — water: tap water in Japan is excellent and safe everywhere

## Eating Customs at a Glance

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Say いただきます / ごちそうさまでした | Stick chopsticks upright in rice |
| Slurp noodles | Pass food chopstick-to-chopstick |
| Pour drinks for others | Pour your own drink (let others do it) |
| Try everything | Waste food if you can avoid it |
| Eat with both hands (hold bowl up) | Walk and eat in public (considered rude) |
