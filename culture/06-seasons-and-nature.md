# Seasons & Nature — 四季と自然

> *"Japan experiences the seasons with an intensity that shapes language, food, clothing, greetings, decor, festivals, and poetry. Learning seasonal vocabulary isn't optional — it's structural."*

Japan's four seasons (四季 — shiki) are woven into the fabric of daily life in a way that surprises many visitors. Seasonal references appear in everything from formal letter openers to ramen menus. The Japanese word **旬 (shun)** — peak season for food, the perfect moment — captures this perfectly.

---

## 春 — Haru (Spring)

**Months:** March, April, May

Spring is the season of new beginnings: the school year starts, companies' fiscal years begin, cherry blossoms bloom, and the air shifts.

### Spring Vocabulary
| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 桜 | sakura | cherry blossom |
| 花見 | hanami | cherry blossom viewing |
| 春一番 | haruichiban | First strong south wind of spring |
| 入学式 | nyūgakushiki | School entrance ceremony |
| 卒業式 | sotsugyōshiki | Graduation ceremony |
| 霞 | kasumi | spring haze / mist |
| うぐいす | uguisu | bush warbler (its call is the sound of spring) |
| たんぽぽ | tanpopo | dandelion |
| 菜の花 | nanohana | rapeseed flower (bright yellow fields) |
| つくし | tsukushi | horsetail plant (edible spring vegetable) |

### Spring in Language
Traditional letter openings for spring:
> 春暖の候、いかがお過ごしでしょうか。
> "In this warm spring season, how have you been?"

The word **春めく (harumeku)** — "to become spring-like" — captures the feeling when spring arrives.

**花吹雪 (hanafubuki)** — "flower blizzard" — the shower of cherry blossom petals falling in the wind. One of the most beautiful words in Japanese.

## 夏 — Natsu (Summer)

**Months:** June, July, August

Japanese summer is hot and humid — especially in Tokyo, Osaka, and the major cities. The rainy season arrives first, then the punishing heat of late July–August.

### Summer Vocabulary
| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 梅雨 | tsuyu / baiu | Rainy season (June–July) |
| 蒸し暑い | mushiatsui | Hot and humid |
| 打ち水 | uchimizu | Splashing water on streets to cool them |
| 夏祭り | natsumatsuri | Summer festival |
| 花火大会 | hanabi taikai | Fireworks festival |
| 盆踊り | bon odori | Obon festival dance |
| 蝉 | semi | cicada — the sound of Japanese summer |
| 蛍 | hotaru | firefly |
| 西瓜 | suika | watermelon |
| かき氷 | kakigōri | shaved ice |
| 浴衣 | yukata | light summer kimono |
| 向日葵 | himawari | sunflower |
| 扇子 | sensu | folding fan |
| うちわ | uchiwa | round fan |
| 熱中症 | necchūshō | heat stroke |
| 台風 | taifū | typhoon |

### The Sound of Summer
The cicada (蝉 — semi) is so strongly associated with summer that 蝉時雨 (semishigure — "cicada shower") — the sound of thousands of cicadas — is a season word (季語) that immediately evokes midsummer.

**梅雨明け (tsuyu ake)** — "end of the rainy season" — is announced by the Japan Meteorological Agency and celebrated like a holiday.

## 秋 — Aki (Autumn)

**Months:** September, October, November

Japan's autumn is spectacular. The maple leaves (紅葉 — kōyō) turn fiery red and gold. The heat breaks, the food peaks, and the air becomes crisp.

### Autumn Vocabulary
| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 紅葉 | kōyō / momiji | Autumn foliage / maple |
| もみじ | momiji | Japanese maple (specifically) |
| 銀杏 | ichō | ginkgo (turns bright yellow) |
| 実り | minori | harvest / fruit bearing |
| 秋晴れ | akibare | clear autumn sky |
| 秋風 | akikaze | autumn breeze |
| 虫の音 | mushi no ne | sound of insects (crickets at night) |
| 松茸 | matsutake | matsutake mushroom (expensive, aromatic, seasonal) |
| 栗 | kuri | chestnut |
| 柿 | kaki | persimmon |
| 梨 | nashi | Japanese pear |
| さんま | sanma | Pacific saury (grilled autumn fish) |
| 月見 | tsukimi | moon-viewing |
| お月見 | otsukimi | October moon-viewing festival |
| 中秋の名月 | chūshū no meigetsu | Harvest moon |
| 衣替え | koromogae | Changing seasonal wardrobe |

### 食欲の秋 (Shokuyoku no Aki)
"Autumn of appetite" — Japanese recognize autumn as the peak season for eating. 芸術の秋 (autumn of the arts), 読書の秋 (autumn of reading), スポーツの秋 (autumn of sports) are the other "autumns." Every pleasant activity becomes "the __ of autumn."

### Tsukimi — Moon Viewing
On the 15th night of the 8th lunar month, Japanese traditionally view the harvest moon, eat 月見団子 (tsukimi dango — rice dumplings) and seasonal foods, and appreciate the moon's beauty. Many ramen shops release 月見ラーメン (egg-topped ramen) during this season.

## 冬 — Fuyu (Winter)

**Months:** December, January, February

Winter brings snow to large parts of Japan (Hokkaido gets enormous snowfall), bare trees, cold winds — and the year's greatest holidays and warmest food.

### Winter Vocabulary
| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 雪 | yuki | snow |
| 初雪 | hatsuyuki | first snow of the season |
| 雪景色 | yukigeshiki | snowy scenery |
| 霜 | shimo | frost |
| 氷 | kōri | ice |
| こたつ | kotatsu | heated table (blanket covers the top — everyone sits underneath) |
| おでん | oden | hot pot dish, winter comfort food |
| 鍋 | nabe | hot pot (general term) |
| ゆず | yuzu | yuzu citrus — fragrant, used at winter solstice baths |
| 冬至 | tōji | winter solstice |
| ゆず湯 | yuzuyu | yuzu-infused bath at winter solstice |
| 年末 | nenmatsu | year end |
| 大晦日 | ōmisoka | New Year's Eve |
| 除夜の鐘 | joya no kane | New Year's Eve bell (rung 108 times at midnight) |
| 初詣 | hatsumōde | first shrine visit of the New Year |
| お正月 | oshōgatsu | New Year |
| お節料理 | osechi ryōri | New Year's traditional food |

### コタツ Culture
The **こたつ (kotatsu)** is a low table with a built-in heater, covered by a thick blanket (布団 — futon). You sit with legs underneath in the warmth. It's an institution:

> コタツから出られない。(Kotatsu kara derarenai.)
> "I can't get out of the kotatsu."
> (Relatable seasonal complaint)

There is also a **こたつ猫** (kotatsu neko) phenomenon — cats that colonize the kotatsu and refuse to share.

## Seasonal Greetings (季節の挨拶)

Japanese people adjust their greetings and letter openings by season. This is considered refined communication:

### Spring
> 春暖の候 / 花の便りも聞こえてくる今日この頃
> "In this warm spring / as word of blooming flowers arrives"

### Summer  
> 盛夏の候 / 猛暑の候
> "In the height of summer / In this fierce heat"

> 暑中お見舞い申し上げます。
> Summer greeting (July–August): "Warm wishes to you in this summer heat"

> 残暑お見舞い申し上げます。
> Late summer greeting (August after Obon): "Warm wishes in this lingering summer heat"

### Autumn
> 秋冷の候 / 紅葉の便りも聞こえる頃
> "As autumn cools / as news of autumn leaves arrives"

### Winter
> 師走の候 / 年の瀬も押し迫って
> "In this end-of-year rush / as year's end approaches"

> 寒中お見舞い申し上げます。
> Winter greeting: "Warm wishes in this cold season"

## 季語 — Kigo (Season Words)

In **俳句 (haiku)**, every poem traditionally contains a **季語 (kigo)** — a season word that places the poem in a specific time of year. Learning kigo is an elegant way to understand seasonal association:

| Season | Kigo | Association |
|---|---|---|
| Spring | 桜 (sakura) | Cherry blossoms |
| Spring | うぐいす (uguisu) | Bush warbler's call |
| Spring | 霞 (kasumi) | Haze |
| Summer | 蛍 (hotaru) | Firefly |
| Summer | 蝉 (semi) | Cicada |
| Summer | 梅雨 (tsuyu) | Rainy season |
| Autumn | 紅葉 (momiji) | Maple leaves |
| Autumn | 名月 (meigetsu) | Harvest moon |
| Autumn | 虫の音 (mushi no ne) | Insect sounds |
| Winter | 雪 (yuki) | Snow |
| Winter | 除夜の鐘 (joya no kane) | New Year's Eve bell |
| Winter | こたつ | Heated table |

## Haiku — A Quick Word

Haiku (俳句) is Japan's most famous poetic form: three lines of 5-7-5 **mora** (sound units). They almost always contain a kigo and a **kireji** (cutting word — a pause or turn).

**Matsuo Bashō's most famous:**
> 古池や　蛙飛び込む　水の音
> (Furuike ya / kawazu tobikomu / mizu no oto)
> Old pond — a frog jumps in — sound of water

The seasons aren't background in Japan. They're foreground.
