# Honorific Japanese — 敬語 (Keigo)

> *"Keigo is where Japanese stops being a language and becomes a performance. Master it and you'll command rooms. Fumble it and you'll apologize profusely — also in keigo."*

敬語 (keigo) is the Japanese system of honorific speech. It's not just "being polite" — it's a grammatically distinct system that encodes the social relationship between speaker, listener, and subject into every verb and noun. It's essential in business, required in formal settings, and a mark of cultural sophistication.

---

## The Three Pillars of Keigo

Keigo has three sub-systems that often work together:

| System | Name | Direction | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **尊敬語** | Sonkeigo | Elevates the OTHER person | "What you do is elevated" |
| **謙譲語** | Kenjōgo | Humbles YOURSELF (or your group) | "What I do is humble" |
| **丁寧語** | Teinego | Adds general politeness | The です/ます system |

The first two are the heart of keigo. They work as a pair:

> When your boss **does something** → use 尊敬語 (elevate their action)
> When **you do something** for your boss → use 謙譲語 (humble your action)

---

## 尊敬語 — Sonkeigo (Elevating Others)

### Special Sonkeigo Verbs

These replace everyday verbs when describing what a superior does:

| Plain verb | Meaning | Sonkeigo form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| いる | be (person) | いらっしゃる | to be (honorific) |
| 行く | go | いらっしゃる | to go (honorific) |
| 来る | come | いらっしゃる | to come (honorific) |
| 食べる | eat | 召し上がる | to eat (honorific) |
| 飲む | drink | 召し上がる | to drink (honorific) |
| 言う | say | おっしゃる | to say (honorific) |
| する | do | なさる | to do (honorific) |
| 知っている | know | ご存知だ | to know (honorific) |
| くれる | give (to me) | くださる | to give (honorific) |
| もらう | receive | いただく | to receive (humble — used in keigo contexts) |
| 見る | see | ご覧になる | to see (honorific) |
| 読む | read | お読みになる | to read (honorific) |
| 思う | think | お思いになる | to think (honorific) |

> **Note:** いらっしゃる handles three verbs (いる、行く、来る). Context determines which.

### The お〜になる Construction

For verbs without a special sonkeigo form, use: **お + verb stem + になる**

| Verb | Sonkeigo construction |
|---|---|
| 待つ (wait) | お待ちになる |
| 帰る (return home) | お帰りになる |
| 使う (use) | お使いになる |
| 書く (write) | お書きになる |
| 持つ (hold) | お持ちになる |

Example:
> 社長はもうお帰りになりました。
> The president has already returned home.

## 謙譲語 — Kenjōgo (Humbling Yourself)

### Special Kenjōgo Verbs

These replace everyday verbs when describing what YOU or your group does toward a superior:

| Plain verb | Meaning | Kenjōgo form |
|---|---|---|
| いる | be (person) | おる |
| 行く | go | 参る (mairu) |
| 来る | come | 参る (mairu) |
| 食べる / 飲む | eat / drink | いただく |
| 言う | say | 申す (mōsu) |
| する | do | いたす |
| もらう | receive | いただく |
| あげる | give (upward) | 差し上げる (さしあげる) |
| 知っている | know | 存じる / 存じ上げる |
| 見る | see | 拝見する (はいけんする) |
| 読む | read | 拝読する (はいどくする) |
| もらう | receive | いただく |
| 聞く | ask / listen | 伺う (うかがう) |
| 訪ねる | visit | 伺う (うかがう) |
| 思う | think | 存じます (ぞんじます) |

Examples:
> 明日、御社に伺います。
> (Ashita, onsha ni ukagaimasu.)
> I will visit your company tomorrow.

> 先ほど田中様よりご連絡をいただきました。
> (Sakihodo Tanaka-sama yori go-renraku wo itadakimashita.)
> I received a message from Tanaka-sama just now.

## 丁寧語 — Teinego (General Politeness)

This is the です/ます system from [Polite Japanese](03-polite-japanese.md), elevated further:

| Standard polite | Elevated teinego |
|---|---|
| です | でございます |
| あります | ございます |
| ありがとうございます | ありがとうございます (same, but said with full weight) |
| すみません | 失礼いたします / 申し訳ございません |
| 分かりました | かしこまりました / 承知いたしました |

### かしこまりました vs. 承知いたしました

Both mean "understood" / "I will take care of that" — but:
- **かしこまりました** — extremely formal, used in high-end service (luxury hotels, department stores)
- **承知いたしました** — formal business Japanese, used in offices and professional emails

Neither sounds odd in context. Both sound far more professional than ただ了解です (which is casual and considered slightly abrupt in business).

## Keigo Vocabulary Pairs

Many nouns also have honorific / humble versions:

| Neutral | Honorific (for others) | Humble (for yourself) |
|---|---|---|
| 名前 (name) | お名前 | 私の名前は |
| 会社 (company) | 御社 (おんしゃ) | 弊社 (へいしゃ) |
| 人 (person) | お方 (おかた) | 者 (もの) |
| 家 (house/home) | お宅 | 自宅 |
| 意見 (opinion) | ご意見 | 私見 |
| 挨拶 (greeting) | ご挨拶 | ご挨拶 (same) |
| 連絡 (contact) | ご連絡 | ご連絡 (same form, humble intent) |
| 確認 (confirmation) | ご確認 | 確認 / ご確認 |
| 説明 (explanation) | ご説明 | ご説明 |
| 報告 (report) | ご報告 | ご報告 |

> **御社 vs. 弊社:**
> Always use 御社 (onsha) for the OTHER company. Always use 弊社 (heisha) for YOUR company. Getting these backwards is a significant error in business Japanese.

## Keigo in Real Situations

### Business Phone Call

> お電話ありがとうございます。株式会社〇〇でございます。
> (Odenwa arigatō gozaimasu. Kabushiki kaisha 〇〇 de gozaimasu.)
> Thank you for calling. This is 〇〇 Inc.

> 少々お待ちいただけますでしょうか。
> (Shōshō omachi itadakemasu deshō ka?)
> Could you please hold for just a moment?

> 田中はただいま席を外しております。
> (Tanaka wa tadaima seki wo hazushite orimasu.)
> Tanaka is currently away from his desk.
> *(Note: No さん for Tanaka — he's uchi, and you're humbling him to the outside caller)*

> 折り返しご連絡させていただきます。
> (Orikaeshi go-renraku sasete itadakimasu.)
> I will have him return your call.

### Business Email (Full Template)

```
件名：〇〇のご確認について

〇〇株式会社
〇〇部　〇〇様

お世話になっております。
株式会社〇〇の田中でございます。

先日はお時間をいただきありがとうございました。
早速ではございますが、ご確認いただきたい件がございます。

[本文]

ご不明な点がございましたら、どうぞお気軽にご連絡ください。
引き続きよろしくお願いいたします。

〇〇株式会社
田中 太郎
```

### Meeting Phrases
> ご説明いただけますでしょうか。
> Could you please explain that?

> おっしゃる通りでございます。
> Exactly as you say / I agree completely.

> 検討させていただきます。
> We will consider it. (Often means "we'll think about a polite way to say no")

> おかげさまで順調に進んでおります。
> Thanks to you, things are proceeding smoothly.

## The Keigo Mistakes That Reveal a Learner

### Mistake 1: Using Keigo for Your Own Group to an Outsider
> ✗ 田中様はいらっしゃいます。 (Elevating your own colleague to an outside party)
> ✓ 田中はおります。 (Humbling your colleague when speaking to outsiders)

### Mistake 2: Double Keigo (二重敬語)
Using two levels of honorific simultaneously:
> ✗ おっしゃられました (おっしゃる already sonkeigo; adding られ is redundant/incorrect)
> ✓ おっしゃいました

### Mistake 3: Using いただく for Third-Party Actions
> ✗ 社長がいただきます (いただく is YOUR humble receiving verb, not for the president)
> ✓ 社長が召し上がります (use sonkeigo for the president's actions)

### Mistake 4: ご〜する vs お〜する
- Chinese-origin words use ご: ご確認、ご連絡、ご説明
- Japanese-origin words use お: お知らせ、お待ち、お届け
- Exceptions exist — learn them case by case

## The Double Humble Construction: させていただく

This construction — literally "allow me to humbly do X" — is extremely common in modern Japanese keigo and deserves special attention:

> 確認させていただきます。
> (Kakunin sasete itadakimasu.)
> Allow me to confirm that. / I will confirm that.

> 出席させていただきます。
> I will humbly attend.

> 休業させていただきます。
> We will be closed. (Literally: we humbly allow ourselves to be closed)

It's everywhere — perhaps overused in modern Japanese, but entirely expected in business settings. Using it signals consideration for the listener.

## Summary: Which Keigo Form to Use?

| Who is acting? | Action toward whom? | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Superior / client | — | 尊敬語 |
| You | Toward superior / client | 謙譲語 |
| Colleague (uchi) | — mentioned to outsider | 謙譲語 (humbling) |
| You | General polite statement | 丁寧語 (でございます) |

When in doubt: if they're doing it, elevate. If you're doing it, humble.
